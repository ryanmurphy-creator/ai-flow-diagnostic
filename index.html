HOW THE AI FLOW FRAMEWORK WORKS

How the AI Flow Framework Works

In the 1970s, psychologist Mihaly Csikszentmihalyi identified Flow: the state where you're so absorbed in a task that everything else disappears. It happens when the complexity of a challenge aligns with your ability to meet it.

This tool applies that principle to working with AI. One axis measures AI Capability, the other measures Task Complexity. The combination places your task in one of 8 zones, from AI Flow to Waste of Time.

AI Capability (horizontal axis)

How good is AI at this type of task right now? Scored across 8 categories using benchmark data from 2025-26:

  • Coding and scripting
  • Summarization and extraction
  • Research and synthesis
  • Data analysis
  • Writing in a specific voice
  • Presentation design
  • Strategic judgment
  • Spatial and physical reasoning

Task Complexity (vertical axis)

How much human judgment does this task require? Assessed across five factors:

  • How many judgment calls are involved
  • Stakeholder impact and stakes
  • Interdependencies with other systems or people
  • Domain expertise and context required
  • Novelty (have you done this before?)

The more specific you are about your task, the more accurate the placement.
